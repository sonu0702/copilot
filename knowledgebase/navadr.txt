Stripe is a technology company that builds economic infrastructure for the internet. Businesses of every size—from new startups to public companies—use Stripe's software to accept payments and manage their businesses online.
Stripe offers a suite of payment APIs that powers commerce for online businesses of all sizes, including fraud prevention, and subscription management.
Stripe's main products include payment processing, billing, connect (for marketplaces), and radar (for fraud detection).
Stripe supports a wide variety of payment methods, including credit cards, debit cards, and popular digital wallets.
Stripe was founded in 2010 by Patrick and John Collison.
Stripe's headquarters are in San Francisco, California, and Dublin, Ireland.
Stripe provides tools for developers to integrate its payment platform into websites and mobile applications.
Key features of Stripe include:
- Online payment processing for various card types (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, etc.)
- Support for international payments and multiple currencies.
- Subscription and recurring billing management.
- Marketplace functionality with Stripe Connect to handle payments for third-party sellers.
- Advanced fraud detection and prevention with Stripe Radar.
- Invoicing capabilities.
- Reporting and analytics dashboards.
- Secure handling of sensitive payment information, complying with PCI DSS standards.
Stripe is known for its developer-friendly APIs and extensive documentation.
Stripe competes with other payment processors like PayPal, Square, and Adyen.
To use Stripe, businesses typically sign up for an account, integrate Stripe's APIs or SDKs into their platform, and configure their payment settings.
Stripe charges fees on a per-transaction basis, which can vary depending on the payment method and other factors.
